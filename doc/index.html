Attachment

Cells start with adhesin molecules that let them attach to the bottom surface. When a cell touches the surface and carries enough adhesins, a PivotJoint pins it in place. Adhesins split between daughters at division, so descendants of an attached lineage gradually exhaust the pool and the youngest cells eventually fail to attach.
